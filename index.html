Welcome!


Hi! I am a prospective PhD student for fall 2024. I recently completed my B.Sc. (Engg.) degree from the University of Chittagong, where I immersed myself in the captivating fields of computer science and engineering. Throughout my undergraduate journey, I discovered a profound interest in several areas, including causal inference, relation extraction, named entity recognition, NLP, and computational linguistics.

Over the past 3+ years, I have been actively involved in groundbreaking research as an Research Assistant in the CSECU-DSG Research Group under the supervision of Dr. Abu Nowshed Chy. This invaluable experience has allowed me to sharpen my analytical skills, collaborate with brilliant minds, and make meaningful contributions to the field.

I am particularly fascinated by the field of causal inference, exploring how we can draw meaningful insights from complex data to understand cause-and-effect relationships. Additionally, I have delved into the domains of relation extraction, named entity recognition (NER), NLP, and computational linguistics, aiming to uncover hidden patterns and extract valuable information from unstructured text. For more details, check my CV or hit me up on my email.
